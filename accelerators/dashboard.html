ForcedField and CacheCow have applied to six accelerators and fellowships across Canada, Europe, and the US. Decisions arrive in April and May 2026. This document profiles each program, maps the thesis fit, and lays out the decision calendar.

Section 5 — Accelerator pipeline

The programs. What they are, and why we fit.

Each program was selected because it addresses a specific gap: capital, compute, credibility, or ecosystem. Together they form a portfolio, not a lottery.

April decision wave

MassChallenge Switzerland Global impact accelerator  ·  Lausanne, Switzerland
Decision: April 2026 Cohort begins: June 2026 Location: Switzerland

About the program

MassChallenge Switzerland is one of the world's most prestigious zero-equity accelerators, operating globally from its Lausanne base. The program focuses on high-impact startups across health, food & ag, energy, and advanced technology — with a strong corporate partner network including Nestlé, Givaudan, and Helvetia.

Why ForcedField fits

CacheCow's agricultural intelligence platform and UltraBand's health monitoring capabilities align directly with the food/ag and health tracks. The Swiss location provides a gateway into European enterprise partnerships and CE marking regulatory pathways — both required for international scaling.

What they offer: Zero equity. Mentorship, corporate partnership access, $1M+ prize pool.
Scale Up Calgary 50 Scale Up Canada  ·  Calgary, Alberta
Decision: April 2026 Cohort begins: May 2026 Location: Calgary, AB

About the program

Scale Up Canada's Calgary 50 program selects 50 high-growth Alberta companies for an intensive scaling program, providing peer cohort support, executive mentorship, and connections to Alberta's innovation funding ecosystem.

Why CacheCow fits

CacheCow Agriculture Inc. is incorporated in Alberta and building its ranch pilot programs across the province. This program provides the home-market anchor: direct access to Alberta Innovates, IRAP networks, and the provincial agricultural ecosystem that are central to CacheCow's non-dilutive funding strategy.

What they offer: Scaling program access, government network, peer cohort. No equity taken.
2048 Ventures Pitch Competition 2048 Ventures  ·  New York, NY
Decision: April 2026 Pitch week: May 27 – June 2, 2026 Location: New York City

About the program

2048 Ventures is a pre-seed VC firm focused on diverse founders and frontier technology. Their pitch competition brings selected founders to New York for a week of investor meetings, workshops, and a final pitch to the investment committee.

Why ForcedField fits

The most immediate capital conversion opportunity in the pipeline. 2048's focus on frontier hardware and AI aligns with the 9-component architecture. A strong pitch week positions ForcedField for a pre-seed round to fund CacheCow pilot deployments and UltraBand prototype completion.

What they offer: Investment consideration. Pre-seed VC — potential $250K–$1M check.

May decision wave

Encode: AI for Science — Cohort 2 Pillar VC × ARIA  ·  United Kingdom (1-year fellowship)
Decision: May 2026 Cohort begins: September 1, 2026 Location: UK — visas supported

About the program

Encode is a highly competitive 12-month AI research fellowship backed by Pillar VC and the UK's Advanced Research and Invention Agency (ARIA). Fellows receive a minimum £100K compute budget, competitive salary, travel stipend, healthcare, and pension. IP stays 100% with the founder.

Why ForcedField fits

ForcedField's movement intelligence platform is an AI-for-science problem at its core: neuromorphic edge AI applied to biological systems at picowatt power budgets. The Innatera T1 + LiquidAI ESS integration, cross-species movement analysis, and spiking neural network research are exactly the class of work Encode funds. The compute budget directly accelerates the training pipeline. IP retention is non-negotiable — Encode guarantees it.

What they offer: £100K+ compute budget  ·  competitive salary  ·  full IP retention  ·  Pillar VC investor introductions.
PACE Fellows PACE Program
Decision: May 2026 Cohort: July 6 – August 1, 2026

About the program

PACE Fellows is an intensive 4-week summer fellowship for early-stage founders. The program combines structured curriculum with mentorship from experienced operators and investors, focused on go-to-market strategy and early traction.

Why ForcedField fits

The July–August timing bridges the April decision wave and the fall program starts. PACE provides focused go-to-market refinement for CacheCow ranch pilot deployment and UltraBand event strategy — the critical execution work before Series A preparation in Q4 2026.

What they offer: Intensive mentorship, GTM coaching, investor network access.
Google.org AI for Science Impact Challenge Google.org  ·  Global
Decision: May 2026 Cohort begins: Fall 2026 Location: Global — remote-first

About the program

Google.org's AI for Science challenge provides grant funding and Google infrastructure — Cloud TPUs, Vertex AI, expert support — to organizations using AI to accelerate scientific breakthroughs. Previous cohorts have focused on climate, health, and agriculture.

Why CacheCow fits

CacheCow's disease prediction platform is a direct AI-for-science application: deploying neuromorphic edge AI to predict bovine illness 3–7 days before symptoms appear, reducing antibiotic use and improving food system sustainability. Google's TPU infrastructure would dramatically accelerate the movement pattern training pipeline. The highest-prestige signal in the portfolio if selected.

What they offer: Grant funding ($1–5M range historically)  ·  Google Cloud credits  ·  AI expert access. No equity.

The portfolio is deliberately complementary.

Scale Up Calgary and MassChallenge provide non-dilutive credibility and government network access. 2048 Ventures is the primary near-term capital conversion event. Encode delivers compute infrastructure and the IP protection that is non-negotiable for a patent-forward company. Google.org is the highest-prestige signal in the portfolio and unlocks Google Cloud infrastructure for the training pipeline. PACE bridges the execution gap between decisions and fall cohort starts. A clean sweep is not the goal — the right three or four acceptances from complementary programs positions ForcedField for a Series A close in Q4 2026 from a position of multi-dimensional validation.

I
Capital type diversification
The six programs span four capital types: VC investment (2048), non-dilutive grants (Google.org, MassChallenge), government ecosystem access (Scale Up Calgary), and compute + salary infrastructure (Encode). No single rejection removes the capital pathway.
II
Geography covers every target market
Switzerland opens Europe. New York opens US venture. UK opens the research and deep-tech ecosystem. Calgary anchors Canada. Each acceptance opens a geographic channel that would otherwise require a separate market-entry effort.
III
IP retention is non-negotiable — and every program respects it
ForcedField's value is inseparable from its 300+ patent roadmap. Every program in this portfolio either takes zero equity (MassChallenge, Scale Up, Google.org) or explicitly guarantees IP stays with the founder (Encode). This was a hard selection criterion.

The realistic best-case outcome is three to four acceptances from complementary programs: one capital event (2048 Ventures), one deep-tech fellowship (Encode), one high-prestige grant (Google.org), and one home-market anchor (Scale Up Calgary). This combination delivers compute infrastructure, investor relationships, non-dilutive funding, and Canadian ecosystem credibility simultaneously — positioning ForcedField for a Series A close in Q4 2026 from a position of demonstrated, multi-dimensional validation.
