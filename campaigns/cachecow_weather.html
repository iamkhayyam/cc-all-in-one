The uncomfortable truth about weather data

The world is drowning in satellite data it cannot trust.

"The licensing value is not the raw observation. It is the right to use verified, ground-anchored intelligence inside forecasts, models, alerts, and decision systems — and CacheCow creates that verification layer at continental scale."

Satellites produce enormous volumes of atmospheric data. Government agencies, regulated industries, and AI model trainers are willing to pay a premium for exactly one thing those satellites cannot provide: proof that the observation was correct. A ground-truth layer is not more data. It is a verification and confidence product — evidence that a satellite-derived observation, classification, or forecast was accurate. That is valuable in any procurement setting where accuracy is auditable.

The network

The world's most distributed rural atmospheric sensor network. Built to tag cattle.

CacheCow was designed as a precision livestock intelligence platform. It is also, incidentally, the architecture for a continental ground-truth observation system. This is not a pivot — it is a second revenue stream that costs nothing incremental to build, because the hardware is already deployed and paid for by the agricultural contract.

Atmospheric sensing

Temperature, pressure, humidity — direct

The ONiO Wave Catcher (Sentinel) measures atmospheric variables continuously. Not inferred. Not modelled. Measured at ground level, physically present in the landscape.

Electromagnetic sensing

Lightning precursors at femtotesla sensitivity

The TMR quantum magnetic sensor detects atmospheric electromagnetic field variations — including storm precursors — at sensitivity levels no traditional met station achieves.

GPS positioning

±2cm absolute position, continuously verified

UWB positioning anchors every observation to a sub-2cm GPS coordinate. Every data point is physically located. Every location is continuously verified. The provenance chain is unbreakable.

Mesh architecture

1.5MB daily digest per 500-cow herd

Nordic BLE mesh coordinates a gossip protocol across the herd. By 05:00 AM, one LoRa burst transmits a complete daily digest — atmospheric observations, anomaly flags, confidence scores. Near-zero communication cost per observation.
